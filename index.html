What Is a Feeling?

Everyone has feelings. Feelings are signals from inside us — from our body, heart, and mind. They are not good or bad — they are messages from inside! Feelings help us understand what we need, what matters to us, and how to take care of ourselves and others. When we learn how to notice and understand our feelings, we gain a powerful skill for life.

🌞

Light & Comfortable

Some feelings feel light and comfortable, like joy, excitement, and calm. They make us feel warm inside!

Heavy & Confusing

The COLOR Method

A simple way to understand what you're feeling and decide what to do next.

C

Check Your Body

Notice what is happening inside you. Children learn to recognize physical sensations such as a tight tummy, a fast heartbeat, shaky legs, or warm cheeks.

O

Observe Your Face

Your expression can give you clues. Children recognize how emotions show up through facial expressions like tight lips, wide eyes, or drooping shoulders. A mirror doesn't judge — it only shows what's there.

L

Label The Feeling

Give your feeling a name. Children learn to name emotions accurately — anxiety, fear, anger, sadness, joy, and more. Naming our feelings helps us understand them better.

O

Open A Choice

Think about what you can do with this feeling. Children discover that emotions do not control their actions, and that they always have choices in how to respond.

R

Respond In A Healthy Way

Choose an action that helps you and others. Children practice healthy responses such as breathing, dancing, drawing, squeezing a stress ball, hugging, or talking to a trusted adult.

By the end of the play, students are able to recall the steps, apply them with confidence, and even guide one another through the process.

About The Play

A live, interactive theatrical experience designed to help elementary school students recognize, understand, and manage their emotions in healthy and age-appropriate ways. Through storytelling, music, movement, and direct audience participation, children learn that all emotions are valid, and that they always have a choice in how they respond.

The Story

The play follows two friends, Danny and Sam, as they prepare a birthday surprise that doesn't go exactly as planned.

What starts as excitement slowly turns into moments of anxiety, anger, sadness, and eventually joy. As emotions become bigger and harder to manage, a magical character — The Mirror of Feelings — comes to life.

The Mirror helps the characters pause and reflect, guiding them step by step to:

  • Notice what is happening in their bodies
  • Observe facial expressions
  • Name emotions correctly
  • Explore healthy ways to express those emotions

The situations in the play are intentionally relatable to children's daily lives: school pressure, making mistakes, forgetting important things, disappointment, and celebration.

By the end, children understand that emotions are not something to fear or suppress, but signals that can guide them when handled properly.

Meet The Characters

Three unforgettable characters who model emotional awareness, communication, empathy, and self-regulation.

Danny

Energetic • Cheerful • Caring

Danny wants everything to be perfect and feels pressure when things might go wrong. Through Danny, children learn how anxiety feels in the body and how calming strategies like breathing can help.

The Mirror of Feelings

Magical • Wise • Friendly

The Mirror senses emotions and helps children understand them. Rather than naming feelings for the characters, the Mirror encourages self-discovery.

Sam

Curious • Expressive • Impulsive

Sam experiences anger, sadness, and joy — learning that even heavy emotions can be released in healthy ways such as movement, hugging, or sharing with trusted adults.

Educational Value for Schools

Teaching emotional intelligence in a way that is concrete, memorable, engaging, and developmentally appropriate.

🏫 Why It Matters

Children experience strong emotions daily, especially in school environments — during exams, social interactions, mistakes, surprises, and changes.

Without proper tools, emotions often turn into behavioral challenges. The Mirror of Feelings addresses this need by teaching emotional intelligence through interactive theater.

The play aligns with Social and Emotional Learning (SEL) principles and supports schools in building emotionally healthy learning environments.

🌟 Key Benefits for Students

  • Develops emotional awareness and vocabulary
  • Encourages empathy and understanding of others
  • Teaches self-regulation and calming strategies
  • Reduces impulsive emotional reactions
  • Strengthens communication skills
  • Reinforces trust in adults as emotional support
